# Privacy Policy for SlackGuard

**Last Updated: March 2026**

At SlackGuard, we believe in the same privacy we help you protect. This policy explains how our browser extension handles your information.

---

### 1. Data Collection

SlackGuard **does not collect, store, or transmit** any personal data.

- We do not read your messages.
- We do not see your contact lists.
- We do not track your browsing history.

---

### 2. Local Processing

All analysis performed by SlackGuard happens entirely within your local browser. The extension reads workspace metadata (such as plan type and export settings) only to display the risk banner to you. This data never leaves your computer.

---

### 3. Third-Party Sharing

Since we do not collect any data, we have nothing to share, sell, or trade with third parties. We do not use any third-party analytics or tracking scripts.

---

### 4. Permissions Use

- **Storage:** Used only to remember if you have dismissed the warning banner so it doesn't reappear until you want it to.
- **Tabs:** Used to send messages between the extension popup and your active Slack tab.
- **WebNavigation:** Used to detect when you switch between different Slack channels so the tool can re-scan your privacy settings.
- **Notifications:** Used only to alert you when a workspace scan is complete (optional).

---

### 5. Changes to This Policy

If we update the extension in a way that changes how it handles data, we will update this policy accordingly. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this page will always reflect the most recent revision.
